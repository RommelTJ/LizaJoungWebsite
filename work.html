KnitCount App

"Why would I want to use an ugly tool while knitting beautiful items?" I thought as I looked through the row counters on the App Store. The apps available were not visually appealing or did not include basic row counter functions such as a reset button or the ability to save multiple projects. I wanted to create a product that was simple and engaging, thus the KnitCount app was born.

I started by researching all of the row counters that were available and thorougly diagnosed the issues I had and aspects I liked for each app. I then detailed which items were absolutely necessary for a most viable product (MVP) and came up with a wireframe to visualize the app. I wanted to focus on keeping the app as simple as possible and rigorously applied constraint on applying features that weren't necessary to the essence of the app.

Once the foundation of the app was complete, the design gave way to colors, backgrounds, and images. I wanted to ensure that the app was engaging and decided to include knitting items into the design of the app. The background is a vague representation of knit stitches and the actual counters are placed on balls of yarn.

The app was accepted and successfully launched in the App Store. I learned how to compromise specific design features due to time restrictions. Buttons were changed and the font may be a bit hard to read with the current set up, but those are minor cosmetic changes that can be made with the next release. Overall the app gave me the skills necessary to think about solutions in a mobile-first frame of mind which is currently being utilized on other app designs.
